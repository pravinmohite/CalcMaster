About Period Tracking

Understanding your menstrual cycle helps you:

  • Predict your next period and prepare accordingly
  • Identify your fertile window if you're trying to conceive
  • Know when to expect PMS symptoms
  • Track cycle irregularities that may indicate health concerns

This calculator provides estimates based on your inputs. Every body is different, and cycles can vary due to stress, lifestyle changes, medications, or health conditions.

Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

Menstrual Phase

Day 1-5: The uterine lining sheds, resulting in menstrual flow. This marks the beginning of a new cycle.

Follicular Phase

Day 1-13: Overlaps with menstruation. Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) stimulates follicle growth in the ovaries.

Ovulation

Day 14 (in a 28-day cycle): A mature egg is released from the ovary and moves toward the fallopian tube.

Luteal Phase

Day 15-28: The follicle transforms into the corpus luteum, which produces progesterone to prepare for possible pregnancy.

Tracking for Fertility

If you're trying to conceive, your most fertile days are the 3-5 days before ovulation and the day of ovulation itself. Tracking your cycle can help identify this window.

Remember that ovulation usually occurs around 14 days before your next period starts, not necessarily on day 14 of your cycle.

Cycle Irregularities

It's normal for cycles to vary by a few days from month to month. However, consistently irregular cycles may indicate hormonal imbalances, stress, excessive exercise, significant weight changes, or conditions like PCOS.

Consult a healthcare provider if:

  • Your periods suddenly become irregular after being regular
  • You miss three or more periods in a row
  • Your periods are less than 21 days or more than 35 days apart
  • Your periods last longer than 7 days
  • Your periods are unusually heavy or painful
